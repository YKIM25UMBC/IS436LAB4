Bachelor of Science in Information Systems

Gain an in-depth technical and conceptual understanding of the IS field. Learn to design, build, and manage computer systems while developing essential programming, database management, and web design skills.

Program Overview

The B.S. in Information Systems is for students who want an in-depth technical and conceptual understanding of the IS field. You will leave UMBC with technical skills like programming, database management, and web design, as well as the business skills required to help you excel in your career.

Our program prepares students for all potential career positions in the information systems field. Learn how to design, build, and manage the computer systems that we depend on in our everyday lives.

For successful completion of the degree, all students must satisfy the general education requirements that are explained in the UMBC Undergraduate Catalog.

Career Opportunities Include:

Systems Analysis and Design

Computer Networking

Health Care Informatics

Database Administration

Information Systems Manager

Business Systems Analyst

Gateway Requirements

Before being allowed to take IS 410 and 450 (and then the rest of the IS 400-level core courses), students must first complete the following gateway requirements:

A grade of "C" or higher in:

  • MATH 155 – Elementary Calculus
  • IS 147 – Introduction to Computer Programming

And a grade of "B" or higher in:

  • IS 300 – Management Information Systems
  • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts

Required Courses

Information Systems Core

  • IS 300 – Management Information Systems
  • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts
  • IS 410 – Introduction to Database Design
  • IS 420 – Database Application Development
  • IS 425 – Decision Support Systems
  • IS 436 – Structured Systems Analysis & Design
  • IS 450 – Data Communications and Networks
  • IS 451 – Network Design and Management
  • IS 3xx or 4xx – Upper Level IS Elective

Mathematics and Computer Science

  • MATH 155 – Applied Calculus
  • MATH 215 – Finite Mathematics for Information Sciences
  • STAT 351 – Applied Statistics for Business and Economics
  • IS 147 – Introduction to Computer Programming or CMSC 201 – Computer Science I for Majors
  • IS 247 – Computer Programming II or CMSC 202 – Computer Science II for Major
  • One additional approved programming course: IS 246, IS 413, IS 448, IS 471, or IS 472

Administrative Science

  • MGMT 210 – The Practice of Management
  • ECON 101 – Principles of Microeconomics (SS)
  • ECON 102 – Principles of Macroeconomics (SS)
  • ECON 121 – Principles of Accounting I
  • ECON 122 – Principles of Accounting II
  • ENGL 393 – Technical Communication or IS 369 – Research Seminar: Writings In Information Systems

Upper-Level Electives (Examples)

  • IS 303, IS 304, IS 460, IS 461, IS 427, IS 428
  • IS 430, IS 438, IS 464, IS 471, IS 472
  • IS 451U, IS 451M, IS 452
  • See catalog for a full listing of available electives

Important Program Requirements

  • All Information Systems majors must complete at least 9 upper level IS and Management courses taught by the IS department at UMBC.
  • Students must earn a grade of "C" or better for any course to be applied to the Information Systems major.
  • IS 300 and IS 310 must be completed in residence and Information Systems does not support third time repeat petitions for the purposes of continuing in this major.
